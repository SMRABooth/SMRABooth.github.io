STRABooth: Spatial-Temporal Representation Alignment
for Customized Video Generation

Anonymous Authors
Paper Code

Abstract

Customized video generation aims to produce videos that faithfully preserve the subject's appearance from reference images while maintaining temporally consistent motion from reference videos. Existing methods focus on preserving local information for spatial and motion features but lack the capability to effectively capture and control global subject identity and coherent motion patterns. To address this, we propose \(\textbf{STRABooth}\), a novel framework that pioneers global representation alignment with stage-by-stage customization for DiT-based architectures. Our approach is structured in three core stages: (1) Global Spatial Control: We leverage global representations from reference images via a self-supervised vision encoder to guide spatial representation alignment, enabling the model to capture the subject's global structure and improve high-level semantic consistency beyond local details. (2) Global Temporal Control: We utilize global temporal representations of optical flow from reference videos to capture structurally coherent and object-level motion trajectories independent of appearance. (3) Decoupling Integration: We propose a spatial-temporal association decoupling strategy that applies sparse LoRAs injection across both locations and timing, effectively reducing interference between spatial and temporal LoRAs. Extensive experiments show that STRABooth excels in subject and motion customization, maintaining consistent subject appearance and motion patterns, proving its effectiveness in controllable text-to-video generation.

Overall Framework of STRABooth

Overview of STRABooth. Our method splits customized video generation into two stages: subject learning and motion learning. Subject learning aligns global spatial features from the vision encoder to enhance fidelity, while motion learning utilizes temporal motion representations from the optical flow encoder to guide motion generation. The pretrained video diffusion model remains frozen during training, and LoRAs are merged at inference to generate customized videos. For simplicity, text input is omitted from the figure.
